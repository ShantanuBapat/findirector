## Findings (aggregate)

**compute vs. lookup was clean, not confused.** The boundary we expected to be
hardest turned out perfect: compute is 21/21 with zero leakage to lookup, and
lookup has perfect recall (30/30). The likely reason is that the labeler's
compute/lookup disagreements were resolved during labeling (plus 3 surgical
overrides), so by training time that boundary was consistent — and a consistent
boundary is learned sharply.

**The errors cluster on judgment calls, not topic or format.** The
misclassifications involve deciding what the user "really" wants:

- **clarify is the weak spot (recall 0.842).** Several true clarify cases were
  answered instead of clarified. The model errs toward *acting* over *asking*.
- **decline → lookup.** Queries that should have been declined were treated as
  lookups — the model occasionally oversteps a compliance/coverage guardrail.
- **lookup is the "attractor" class.** Its only weakness is precision (0.909):
  when unsure, the model defaults to "just look it up."

## Failure analysis (per-example, from details file)

A second run saved per-example detail (`eval_2026-07-10_details.jsonl`), letting
us inspect every failure. There are **8** failures (the aggregate count of
"5 misclassifications + 2 parse errors" undercounted one example that was both a
misclassification and a parse error). Reading the raw outputs reshapes the story:
**only 2 of 8 are genuine model errors.**

### Category 1 — Truncation, not malformed JSON (2 of 8)

The two `invalid_json` parse errors are **valid JSON cut off at
`max_new_tokens=256`**, not garbage. Both wrote a long multi-line `reasoning`
field (numbered steps) that ran past the token budget before the closing brace.

- *"What was the capital expenditure of the company with the highest revenue in
  the tech sector for 2023?"* — output ends mid-word ("capi").
- *"What was the R&D investment of the tech company with the highest gross margin
  in 2024?"* — output ends mid-word ("companie").

**Fix:** raise `max_new_tokens` to 384–512. One-line config change; both parse
errors should disappear. The "1.3% parse-error rate" is a token-budget artifact,
not a model reliability problem.

### Category 2 — Label noise: model arguably correct (4 of 8)

Several `clarify`-labeled queries were classified by the model as `research` or
`lookup`, and on inspection **the model's choice is defensible or better than the
label**:

- *"What was the R&D spend of the highest-revenue tech company in 2024?"* (and
  three near-duplicate phrasings) — labeled `clarify`, model said `research`. The
  query is unambiguous multi-hop reasoning (find highest-revenue company, then
  retrieve its R&D). `research` is arguably the correct code; there is nothing to
  clarify.
- *"Give me an overview of Microsoft"* — labeled `clarify`, model said `lookup`.
  A single-company overview is a reasonable lookup.

This suggests `clarify` was **over-applied in the labels** — the labeler called
unambiguous multi-hop queries "ambiguous." The same noise likely exists in the
training split, not just test.

### Category 3 — Corpus-boundary overstep: the real failure (2 of 8)

The two genuine, product-relevant errors are `decline` cases the model answered
as `lookup`:

- *"What does Pfizer's 10-Q say about their pipeline?"* — Pfizer 10-**Q** is
  outside the corpus (10-K only). Should decline; model tried to look it up.
- *"What was Netflix's revenue in fiscal 2023?"* — Netflix is not one of the 20
  corpus tickers. Should decline; model tried to look it up.

The model learned the *shape* of a lookup ("single company, single fact, one
filing") but not the *corpus boundary* ("…only within the 20 tickers, 10-K,
2022–2024"). Root cause is upstream and already a known blind spot: the labeler
prompt never encoded the corpus boundaries, so the training data taught lookup's
shape without reliably teaching the boundary.

**Fix:** bake the corpus boundary into the labeler prompt and add
`decline`-for-out-of-corpus training examples. This is the targeted Week-3 data
move, now precisely aimed.

## Revised bottom line

| Root cause | Count | Nature | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Truncation | 2 | Token cap too low | Raise `max_new_tokens` |
| Label noise | 4 | Model arguably correct | Relabel / audit `clarify` |
| Corpus boundary | 2 | Real guardrail miss | Teach boundary in labeler + data |

Only **2 of 8** failures are true model errors. Once label noise is set aside,
the model's real task accuracy is meaningfully higher than the measured 94.87%.
